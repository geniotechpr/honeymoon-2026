Afternoon — Settle In

Arrive by midday. Airport SUV transfer to the Waldorf (~45 min). Golf cart orientation, then explore the pools — find your favourite spot together.

Sunset — Peacock Alley

Costa Rica sunsets fall 5–6 PM year-round. Be at Peacock Alley — the Waldorf's signature cocktail lounge — by 5pm with a drink in hand.

Dinner — La Finca

All beef on property is wagyu. The steak and the heirloom tomato tomatada are standouts. Your first evening as newlyweds in paradise.

Full Day — Hacienda Guachipelín

Hotel-arranged transport (~45 min). Canyon ziplining and rappelling through Río Blanco Canyon, 5km river tubing, horseback riding, Costa Rican buffet lunch with live marimba, and Rio Negro hot springs with volcanic mud baths. Plan for 8–9 hours — one of the best adventure days in all of Costa Rica.

🌧 Activities run all morning. Afternoon rain during hot springs is actually pleasant — warm volcanic pools in the rain are extraordinary.

Morning — Drive to Río Celeste

Private driver (~2 hours from Liberia area). ⚠️ Tenorio Volcano National Park tickets must be pre-purchased via the SINAC website — timed entry, no walk-ups.

Midday — The Trail

3–4 hours for the waterfall, Mirador viewpoint, Laguna Azul, the Borbollones boiling spring, and Teñideros — where two clear rivers merge and the water turns impossibly blue. Lunch outside the park.

Evening — Arrive La Fortuna

2 more hours to La Fortuna. Check in and head straight to the thermal pools. Soaking in volcano-heated water at dusk after a day of hiking — the ideal arrival ritual.

🌧 Ask your driver to check river conditions that morning. Heavy rain can turn the blue water grey-brown. If so, take the direct route and revisit as a day trip.

7–9am — Místico Hanging Bridges

Guided tour during prime wildlife hours — spider monkeys, howler monkeys, exotic birds, pit vipers. Early morning gives the best Arenal Volcano views before clouds roll in.

Midday — La Fortuna Waterfall

230 feet cascading into a turquoise pool, reached by 500 steep steps. Bring swimsuits. Allow 2–3 hours including the swim. Unforgettable together.

Evening — Hot Springs at Dusk

Return to your resort for thermal pools with Arenal as the backdrop. The swim-up bar at sunset is particularly magical.

🌧 Do the waterfall before afternoon rain. The pool is beautiful in light rain — avoid heavy downpours when steps get slippery.

Morning — Wildlife Walk

Guided tour for sloths, poison dart frogs, iguanas, and dozens of bird species. In rainy season, sloths are more active foraging for fresh leaves — June is excellent for this.

Midday — Lake Arenal

Scenic boat or kayak tour with the volcano rising behind the lake. Lunch at a lakeside restaurant. Pure romance.

Afternoon — Rafting or Spa

White-water rafting on the Arenal River (Class III, ~2 hours) — rivers are fuller in June. Or: spa and a final long soak in the hot springs together.

What to Expect
June Weather

Mid-June is Costa Rica's "green season." The pattern: mornings are clear and beautiful, rain arrives 2–4pm and passes in 1–2 hours. The entire itinerary is built to work with this rhythm.

☀️
Guanacaste
Driest part of the country. Hard rains don't start until August. Your Waldorf days will likely be mostly dry.
🌤
La Fortuna
Sunny mornings with good Arenal views. Afternoon/evening rainstorm for an hour or two. All activities planned for mornings.
🌧 Río Celeste watch: Heavy rain in preceding days can turn the blue river grey-brown. Have the driver check conditions the morning of Day 4.
Be Prepared
Packing List

🌧 Rain Essentials

  • Lightweight packable rain poncho (skip umbrella)
  • Quick-dry everything — no cotton
  • Waterproof sandals/shoes
  • Dry bag / waterproof pouch for phone & camera
  • Rain cover for daypack

☀ Heat & Humidity

  • Sunscreen (UV is intense even with clouds)
  • DEET / picaridin insect repellent
  • Microfiber towel
  • Electrolyte packets
  • Refillable water bottle (tap water is safe!)

👔 Clothing

  • Shorts & t-shirts (you'll live in these)
  • One smart dinner outfit for Waldorf
  • Light fleece for La Fortuna evenings
  • Swimsuits (plural — nothing dries fast enough)

💚 Next Time — Monteverde Cloud Forest

Despite being only 15km from La Fortuna as the crow flies, Monteverde takes 3.5–4 hours by road. Adding it would mean cutting La Fortuna short or extending to 8–9 days. If you return, two nights there adds cloud forest bridges, Central America's longest zipline, and the Continental Divide atmosphere. The perfect excuse for trip number two.
